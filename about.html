Who We Are

Since our founding in 2015, Coltivare has focused on one simple mission: helping farmers grow smarter and more sustainable crops and livestock. What began as a small team of agronomists and aeronautical engineers from Purdue University researching drone automation and computer vision has grown into a trusted provider of innovative products and services for the agriculture industry.

Today, Coltivare continues that mission by delivering cutting-edge precision agriculture tools and services. Leveraging our core competencies in drones and computer vision, we help farmers detect issues early—before they escalate—and minimize waste. Our drones and proprietary software enable farmers to more accurately monitor crops and herds and identify key problems such as: water and heat stress, disease, soil erosion, projected yields, and more.

In our first 10 years of operations Coltivare has already become a trusted partner for thousands of farmers providing them with tools and services that enable them to have data driven insights that empower them to optimize farm operations, reduce waste, and drive improvements in sustainability. As we look towards the next decade of operations for our company we remain committed to helping farmers grow smarter and more sustainable crops and livestock by driving innovation in agriculture.

Our Management Team



CEO and Founder
Jane Perdue

Jane has had a lifelong passion for agronomy that began as a child when she would visit her grandfather's farm in Northwest Indiana. She has pursued that passion throughout her life by participating in 4H and working for her grandfather at the family farm each summer from 6th to 12th grade. She then went on to pursue her education at Purdue University where she would eventually become a professor in the Purdue University College of Agriculture where she examined and taught about the intersection of technology and agriculture. Coltivare was born out of this research which she was undertaking with funding from the USDA. While Jane did leave her position at Purdue in 2015 to run the company full time, she is still a frequent guest speaker in classes and events.

Education:

  • Purdue University: BS in Agronomy '06
  • Purdue University: MS in Agronomy '08
  • Purdue University: PhD in Agronomy '11

CTO
Daniel Mitchells

Daniel Mitchells is an expert in drones and camera equipment who has served as Coltivare's CTO since its founding in 2015. His interest and passion in photography began as a child when his parents gave him a toy camera for his third birthday. As he grew older a new passion for aviation and aeronautics emerged the stories his grandfather told him about being a pilot in Korea. The marriage of both of these interests came naturally in modern drones which he learned both how to use and how to design while he was an aerospace engineering student at Purdue University. It was at Purdue during his work on his masters degree that he met Jane Perdue who brought him into her research on technology and agriculture. This fruitful partnership led to Coltivare as we know it today.

Education:

  • Purdue University: BS in Aerospace Engineering '12
  • Purdue University: MS in Aerospace Engineering '14

He spent several years hopping around various startups before settling in for two 4 year stints at Google and Meta.
